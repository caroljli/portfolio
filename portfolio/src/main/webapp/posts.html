PHILLY IN QUARANTINE

And Little Things I Appreciate   ‚Ä¢  May 27, 2020

During the semester, I would always complain that I didn't have time to explore Philadelphia enough; despite having been here for the past two years, I felt like I knew very little of what the city had to offer (aside from the occassional restaurant week or food excursion, to say the least).

Philadelphia is very different than the rainy hometown of Vancouver I lived in for over 10 years of my life. Its constant sunshine, early mornings and late nights, summer humidity, and windy tunnels are definitely new experiences to me all-around, coming from the mild, temperate, and wet west coast that I was so familiar with. I always found it very fascinating to go home to find that it was a lot darker and rainier in Vancouver than I had remembered, especially in contrast with the truly always-sunny Philadelphia.

One of my favourite places in the city (near campus, at least) is Cira Green, as I can go and see the entire skyline at once. It feels nice to be able to watch the sunset from an elevated place, taking advantage of the relatively flat suburbs and otherwise lack of elevations, very unlike home. The broad, clear skies means that I can always see the stars. Recently, I've also come to appreciate going to vast, open, outdoor parks like Bellmont Plateau. The Schuylkill River is, however, still crowded with people, rendering it a relatively unsafe place to spend a lot of time at.

SOPHOMORE YEAR 0:

Things I Did that I Often Overlook   ‚Ä¢  May 28, 2020

I think it's interesting how fast a college year can pass‚Äîneedless to say, it often feels like just yesterday that it was the summer before freshmen year, and I was excited to explore all sorts of ~ college novelties ~, whether that be finally moving away from home, living by myself (sort of), or meeting new (and ~ exotic ~) Americans. ;)

And now, living the reality of becoming an upperclassman and attempting to stomach that college is already half over (yikes), I'm wondering what it is that I actually did during the past two years. Amidst the many problem sets and late nights, spending time with people I care about definitely highlights much of my college experience so far and is something I definitely missed in the latter half of the semester altogether. Out of all things that I wish happened this semester, I wanted the second part of sophomore spring to happen, as it ended up being one of the most highly anticipated time periods of my entire college career.

I think the thing about college is that time seems to be condensed. Little of it is mundane, as there's always something to see or someone to meet, and you feel like you have to pack a lot of experiences together in order to meet the pressure of being "sceney" or active in your social life. Although I gave into that pressure a lot during freshmen year (a phenomenon almost directed linked to PennFace, which is basically what Penn calls duck syndrome), I think that, regardless of feeling like you have to be social, you feel bad when you quickly lose touch of friends that you made just as quickly. In a nutshell, I think quarantine has made me pretty unsocial, but I'm excited to appreciate meeting with people again (if this all ends).
